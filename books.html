Project Leadership
Führungsverhalten in agilen und konventionellen Projekten - eine Studie

Abstract
This book focuses on evolution of project management. Several studies identify the lack of social skills as most serious problem in project management today. Within this context, we examine the influence of social psychology to project management. Starting as a technical and engineering dominated science, project management evolves to a holistic discipline, called Project Leadership. We give an overview of major social psychological influences. The research areas leadership theory, motivation theory, and communication theory are presented in detail. We carry out an empirical study about interaction between management model and leadership behaviour of project managers. It is shown that project management models have an significant impact. Depending on the model different skills and competencies are necessary to succeed. It is proven that in agile projects soft skills are more important (in terms of project success) than in conventionally managed projects. Moreover it is shown that in agile projects soft skills are even more important than hard skills.
